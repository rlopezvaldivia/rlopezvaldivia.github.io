I was born and raised in Guadalajara, Jalisco, Mexico. I studied my M.Sc. and Ph.D. in Astrophysics at the Instituto Nacional de Astrofísica, Óptica y Electrónica in Mexico.
Since 2017, I am a postdoctoral fellow at The University of Texas at Austin working with the Immersion Grating Infrared Spectrometer (IGRINS) team.

My research interests lie within the stellar physics field, some included topics are:

  • Young Stellar Objects
  • Solar-like stars
  • Atmospheric parameters
  • Chemical abundances
  • Transiting exoplanets

Currently, I am working on the characterization of young stellar objects through high-resolution infrared spectra obtained with IGRINS at three different facilities: McDonald Observatory (Texas), Lowell Discovery Telescope (Arizona) and Gemini South Telescope (Chile).
